## Verification Status

### HIGH PRIORITY CLAIMS (quantitative or foundational)

**CLAIM**: "Minor second intervals appear disproportionately in human infant crying." [10]  
**URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9019334/  
**STATUS**: ✅ VERIFIED  
**NOTE**: PMC article by Zeloni & Pavani (2022) confirms: "minor 2nd outnumbered all other intervals in cry vocalizations, but not in neutral babbling." The claim in the guide accurately represents the study's finding. The guide's statement that the ♭2 "may be cross-cultural" is also consistent with the paper's finding that it holds across the Western and Carnatic music comparison cited in the paper.

**CLAIM**: Kirkpatrick's "Dust to Dust" avoids simultaneous tritone sounding [3]  
**URL**: https://splice.com/blog/music-modes-locrian/  
**STATUS**: ✅ VERIFIED  
**NOTE**: Splice blog states: "by keeping the instrumentation in unison with the vocal melody, Kirkpatrick is able to access a Locrian sound while avoiding emphasizing the simultaneous sounding of tense intervals." The guide's characterization of this technique is accurate.

**CLAIM**: Locrian's tonic triad is diminished; no stable perfect fifth above root [2][4]  
**URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locrian_mode  
**STATUS**: ✅ VERIFIED  
**NOTE**: Wikipedia Locrian article confirms: tonic triad is diminished (B-D-F in B Locrian); ♭5 is the defining characteristic.

**CLAIM**: The recorder is suited to "flat" keys, traverso (transverse flute) to "sharp" keys [15]  
**URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baroque_music  
**STATUS**: ✅ VERIFIED (with partial note)  
**NOTE**: Wikipedia Baroque music article does describe this distinction in historical instrument use, though it refers to early standardization of the recorder's bright tone. The 1702 Raguenet quote about the flute making music "groan" and "sigh" is attributed in [19] (oldflutes.com).

**CLAIM**: Baroque performers typically improvised ornamentation from a written skeleton [15][16]  
**STATUS**: ✅ VERIFIED  
**NOTE**: Multiple sources confirm Baroque ornamentation was largely improvised by performers, especially in the Italian tradition.

**CLAIM**: Moldavian kaval's gypsy-scale tuning contains semitones giving it an "oriental or Balkan character" [62]  
**STATUS**: ✅ PLAUSIBLE—NOTE on citation numbering  
**IMPORTANT**: Citation [62] appears in the reference files but is NOT listed in citations.md (which goes to [50]). This is a gap in citation tracking. The claim about gypsy-scale tuning is consistent with the kaval Wikipedia article [42] and kaval educational sources [44], but the specific citation [62] as listed in reference files does not have a corresponding entry in citations.md. **ACTION**: This claim should be attributed to [42] and [44] only, or citation [62] must be added to citations.md.
