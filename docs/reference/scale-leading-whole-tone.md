The third mode of the melodic minor scale. Most of the scale moves in whole steps (like a whole-tone scale), but the seventh is raised to create a leading tone pull toward the octave.

**Emotional character**: floating like a whole-tone scale but with a sudden jolt of urgency at the raised seventh. Sounds like a dream that suddenly remembers it needs to end.

**Compositional use**: effective for passages of suspended wonder (whole-tone quality) that need to resolve dramatically (the leading tone). The scale is most interesting when the listener first accepts the whole-tone floating and then is surprised by the leading tone's pull.
