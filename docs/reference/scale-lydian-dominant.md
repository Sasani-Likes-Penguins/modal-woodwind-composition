## What Is Lydian Dominant?

Lydian Dominant combines the raised fourth of Lydian with the flat seventh of Mixolydian—taking the floating quality of Lydian and adding an unresolved, bluesy tension [19][83]. It is the fourth mode of the melodic minor scale.

**C Lydian Dominant**: C – D – E – F♯ – G – A – B♭ – C

Formula: 1 – 2 – 3 – ♯4 – 5 – 6 – ♭7

The F♯ (raised fourth) floats; the B♭ (flat seventh) pulls without urgently resolving. Together, they create a scale that feels simultaneously expansive and slightly dangerous—a tension that hovers rather than resolves.

## The Emotional Territory

Lydian Dominant is the sound of something large, slightly ominous, and beautiful at the same time. It appears in:

- **Film scoring**: Hans Zimmer and other film composers use it for ancient, alien, or mysteriously vast settings [19]
- **Jazz**: used over dominant seventh chords with sharp-eleven alterations—the "altered dominant" sound
- **Scriabin's "Prometheus" / "Mystic chord"**: Scriabin's famous mystic chord, when collapsed into a scale, approximates Lydian Dominant [85][89]—the scale of cosmic mystery and symbolist transcendence

The combination of raised fourth (floating upward) and flat seventh (pulling without fully landing) creates an emotional state of suspended awe—not pure wonder (Lydian) and not pure unresolved tension (Mixolydian) but both simultaneously.

## Compositional Approach

The raised fourth is the "floating" note; the flat seventh is the "unresolved" note. Use them in alternation:

- Approach the raised fourth from below (3 → ♯4) for a moment of lift
- Move from the flat seventh back to the root (♭7 → 1) for a sense of settling without resolution
- The tritone between the root and raised fourth (C → F♯) creates the characteristic "Lydian Dominant tension"—harmonic ambiguity that feels large rather than threatening

**Exercise on alto recorder**: C Lydian Dominant. Play C – E – F♯ (pause, float on the raised fourth) – G – A – B♭ (pause, the unresolved seventh) – C. The two pauses are the emotional content.

---

## Instrument Access

Fully chromatic instruments (recorder, bawu, kaval) access Lydian Dominant in any key. NAF and whistle require chromatic extensions for the raised fourth.
