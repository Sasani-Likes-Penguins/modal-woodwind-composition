## Romanian Minor

**Formula**: 1 – 2 – ♭3 – ♯4 – 5 – 6 – ♭7

**D Romanian Minor**: D – E – F – G♯ – A – B – C – D

Romanian Minor is essentially Dorian with a raised fourth—combining Dorian's "minor with a bright sixth" quality with Lydian's floating raised fourth [83]. This combination is native to Romanian folk music and appears in Bartók's compositions derived from his field recordings.

**Emotional character**: folk-inflected minor with an unusual lift at the raised fourth. The raised fourth (G♯ in D Romanian) creates a moment of unexpected brightness within the minor context—earthy, folk-like, slightly exotic but not aggressively so.

**Kaval connection**: Romanian folk music is part of the kaval's native tradition. Romanian Minor on the kaval is a homecoming.

**Exercise on kaval**: D Romanian Minor. Play D – F – G♯ (the augmented second leap: F → G♯). This ascending gesture—flat third to raised fourth—is the scale's characteristic fingerprint.
