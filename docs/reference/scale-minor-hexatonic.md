## Minor Hexatonic

**Formula**: 1 – ♭3 – 4 – 5 – ♭7 (+ 2)

Minor pentatonic with an added major second—a six-note scale [94][95].

**A Minor Hexatonic**: A – B – C – D – E – G – A

This is natural minor with the flat sixth omitted. The added second (B in A minor hexatonic) gives the scale a "lift" that pure pentatonic minor lacks, while the absence of the flat sixth removes the heaviest note of natural minor.

**Emotional character**: minor but airy; dark but not heavy. The six-note scale sits between the openness of pentatonic and the fuller sound of heptatonic (seven-note) scales. Good for folk-influenced minor melody that needs to breathe.

**On whistle**: the Low D whistle playing E minor hexatonic (E – F♯ – G – A – B – D) is very idiomatic—the six notes correspond cleanly to the instrument's natural scale minus the C natural.
