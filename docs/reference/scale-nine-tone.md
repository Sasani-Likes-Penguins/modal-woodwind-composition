## Nine-Tone Scale (Nonatonic)

**Formula**: various—most commonly the blues nonatonic or the chromatic approach nonatonic

The nine-tone scale adds additional chromatic passing tones to a seven-note scale, creating nine distinct pitch classes within the octave [100]. The most familiar version in blues practice is the combination of major and minor blues scales into a nine-tone collection: 1 – 2 – ♭3 – 3 – 4 – ♭5 – 5 – 6 – ♭7.

**Emotional character**: saturated, chromatic, heavy. The nine-tone scale is less a "scale to play up and down" and more a pitch collection for improvisation—choosing from nine notes based on the emotional moment, with each note available as color or structure.

**For wind players**: the nine-tone blue scale (minor + major blues combined) is most useful as an *approach* vocabulary—the extra notes (♭3 alongside 3, ♭5 alongside 5) are passing colors, not structural notes. Play them between structural tones, let them slide through. The bawu is particularly suited to this approach because its pitch flexibility makes the chromatic passing tones feel organic rather than academic.
