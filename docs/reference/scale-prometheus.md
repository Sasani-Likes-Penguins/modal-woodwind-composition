## Prometheus Scale

**Formula**: 1 – 2 – 3 – ♯4 – 6 – ♭7 (six notes)

**C Prometheus**: C – D – E – F♯ – A – B♭ – C

This six-note scale is associated with Alexander Scriabin's "mystic chord" from his late period and the symphonic poem *Prometheus: The Poem of Fire* [85][89]. It can be thought of as Lydian Dominant without the perfect fifth—the floating raised fourth of Lydian, the unresolved flat seventh of Dominant, and a major sixth.

**Emotional character**: mystical, suspended, cosmic. Scriabin used it to evoke transcendence and spiritual illumination—his late musical language was deeply connected to Theosophist ideas about spiritual evolution. The scale hovers between tonal centers without grounding firmly anywhere.

**Exercise on bawu**: C Prometheus. Play C – E – F♯ (float on the raised fourth) – A – B♭ (float on the flat seventh) – C. The two floating notes (raised fourth and flat seventh) frame the scale's identity. A melody built from approaches to and departures from these two notes captures the Prometheus aesthetic.
