## Neapolitan Major

**Formula**: 1 – ♭2 – ♭3 – 4 – 5 – 6 – 7

**C Neapolitan Major**: C – D♭ – E♭ – F – G – A – B – C

Neapolitan Major is unusual: it has a minor third (E♭) but a major sixth and seventh—making it sound simultaneously dark in its opening and bright in its upper range [81][83][84].

**Emotional character**: complex, bittersweet. The opening (C – D♭ – E♭) sounds Phrygian-dark; the upper range (G – A – B) sounds bright, almost major. A melody that uses the full range of this scale passes through both territories.
